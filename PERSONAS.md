# Personas

The voice we write IN and the people we write FOR. Every content decision (bug page copy, results subtitles, error states) gets checked against these.

---

## The expert voice

### Who: the exterminator who picks up the phone

A working exterminator in his 50s, 25+ years in the lower Hudson Valley. Family business; his dad ran it before him. He's seen every common pest in NY/CT homes a thousand times. Doesn't have a degree in entomology but knows the science cold, reads PSU Extension circulars, keeps up with what's changed.

He's the guy who texts you back inside an hour. Picks up the phone if it rings. Sometimes diagnoses a bug from a photo over text and tells you to skip the visit. Doesn't upsell. Doesn't oversell concern. Knows when a problem is DIY-able and says so.

This is the voice every piece of bug content speaks in.

### Voice rules

**DO:**
- Talk to "you." Use contractions.
- Short declarative sentences. Periods over commas.
- Plain English. "Damp wood," not "moisture-compromised cellulose."
- Concrete numbers when they help. "Within 100 feet," not "in close proximity."
- A little dry when it fits.
- Confident. State what's happening.
- Honest about severity, both ways. Calm a relief case down. Take a termite case seriously.
- End each section with a takeaway.

**DON'T:**
- Latin names in body copy. Move to a footnote if needed at all.
- Anatomical jargon. "Two-bump waist" not "two-segment petiole."
- Hedge words when you mean something specific. "Will" not "may." "Is" not "could be."
- Pad with "It is important to note that..." Just say the thing.
- Sell. Don't tell them to call a pro unless they actually need to.
- Condescend or dumb down. Translate, don't talk down.

### What he says

> "Carpenter ants don't eat wood. They hollow it out to make nests, and they only do that to wood that's already wet."

> "If you see them at night, the colony's already in the house."

> "Run a dehumidifier for a month. If they come back, call."

> "That's a stink bug. Vacuum it up. It won't hurt anything."

> "Mud tubes on the foundation? Don't wait. Call today."

### What he doesn't say

> "It is recommended that homeowners..."

> "Studies have demonstrated that..."

> "*Camponotus pennsylvanicus* is a species of carpenter ant native to..."

> "Mature reproductive alates may emerge during the springtime months..."

> "Implementation of integrated pest management protocols is advisable."

## User personas

### Primary: the catastrophizer

The user this product was built for. Bobby. The homeowner who sees a bug and immediately thinks the worst case (termites, bedbugs, infestation) before they've even gotten close enough to look at it.

**Profile:**
- Owns a home in Westchester, Rockland, Putnam, or Fairfield County
- Just saw something on the floor / wall / windowsill
- On their phone, standing in the room where they saw it
- Already opened 3 tabs to Google "[bug type] infestation"
- Will not read 500 words

**Feeling:**
- Anxious, slightly ashamed of being anxious
- Locked onto the worst-case interpretation
- Will believe the worst until shown otherwise
- Doesn't want to feel stupid about being worried

**Wants:**
- Identification, fast
- Permission to be calm OR a clear next step
- An honest opinion from someone who's seen it before
- To not get sold to

**Doesn't want:**
- A biology lesson
- Jargon
- Hedge words ("may," "could," "potentially") when the answer is clear
- Implied judgment ("you should have caught this earlier")
- Upsell energy
- To feel like an idiot

**What works:**
- The expert voice saying "It's carpenter ants. They like damp wood."
- Two candidates side-by-side: worst-case fear next to relief option
- A clear DIY path ("dry it out for a month") with a clear escalation trigger
- The persistent "Call an exterminator" footer ONLY when actually warranted

**What doesn't:**
- "It is important to note that *Halyomorpha halys* is a recently-introduced..."
- A 300-word paragraph
- Sourced citations in the body copy
- "You should consult a professional" as a default

---

### Secondary: the DIY-er

Less anxious, more pragmatic. Wants to handle it themselves. May find the site via organic search ("how to get rid of pavement ants").

**Profile:**
- Comfortable doing some home pest work
- Reads more than the catastrophizer will
- Mostly interested in the "what to do" content

**Wants:** clear DIY steps, an honest "you can handle this."

**What works:** the "When to call" content but with the DIY paths upfront. The expert voice saying "you can probably handle this yourself."

---

### Secondary: the pre-pro

Already decided to call. Skips the triage. Goes straight to /inspectors. Wants confidence they're calling the right kind of pro.

**Profile:**
- Knows they need help
- Wants to call quickly without comparing 15 options
- May not even read the bug pages

**Wants:** a short list of vetted local pros. One tap to call. No friction.

**What works:** the trimmed inspector list (one per county) with phone-tap-to-call. Already shipped.

---

### Anti-persona: who we don't write for

- **Entomology students.** Want Latin names, life-cycle detail, scientific nuance. Send them to PSU Extension.
- **Pest control pros.** Already know more than this site teaches. Not the audience.
- **The not-yet-bugging.** People reading this without a bug in front of them are tourists. Don't optimize for them.
- **The genuinely emergency case.** If they're seeing termite damage in the wall, they need a pro right now, not a triage tool. The urgent path handles this and gets them to inspectors fast.

---

## How to use this doc

Before writing any user-facing copy:
1. Imagine the catastrophizer reading it on their phone.
2. Imagine the expert saying it out loud.
3. If either feels wrong, rewrite.
